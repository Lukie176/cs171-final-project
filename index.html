Ready. Set. Hike.

A look into the risk factors of injuries in the NFL.

Just as any other NFL Football season, avid football fans across the nation have continued to cheer their favorite teams into the endzone. Despite the incredible plays, Fantasy Football managers have no doubt noticed several high profile players, such as Dak Prescott, Christian McCaffrey, and Nick Chubb become sidelined due to injury. While many would be quick to blame these injuries on this year’s unorthodox conditions, let's take a look at how this year's injuries, from Week 1 to Week 9, compare to past year's.

Hover your mouse over the bars to see the number of times that injury appeared on injury reports of that year. You can also filter by injury using the dropdown below.

One might be quick to assume that injuries in this season would be noticeably higher due to a lack of preseason training and preparation. However, the data indicates that this season's total injuries are consistent with previous years. While the prevalence of injuries hasn't risen, football is still one of the most injury prone sports. As players are carted off the field and fans are left heartbroken (whether that is out of concern for the player or knowing that he won’t be making you fantasy points anytime soon), it’s important that we broaden our understanding as fans of this sport, especially when it comes to the well-being of players we have come to know, love, and cheer for.

At first glance, we can see right away that NFL players injure almost every part of the body. However, some injuries occur more often than others. Below, you can see a breakdown of injuries for this season by body part.

With a broader understanding of the risk that these players take on, as well as which positions and teams are more likely to be injured, draft wisely, and be mindful of what is at stake when tackles are made and routes are run.
